## What is a Variable?

A **variable** is any characteristic or value that can change from one observation to another.

**Examples:**
- Age (20, 25, 30...)
- Height (5'4", 5'8", 6'0"...)
- Gender (Male, Female)
- Income ($30K, $50K, $80K...)

---

## Two Main Types

### 1. Quantitative (Numbers)
*Can be measured with numbers*

| Type | Meaning | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| **Discrete** | Countable, whole numbers | Number of children: 0, 1, 2, 3 |
| **Continuous** | Any value, decimals allowed | Height: 5.5 ft, Weight: 72.3 kg |

### 2. Qualitative (Categories)
*Describes qualities, not numbers*

| Type | Meaning | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| **Nominal** | No order, just names | Color: Red, Blue, Green |
| **Ordinal** | Has order/rank | Rating: Poor, Good, Excellent |
